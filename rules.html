Rules

The Game of Life is played on an infinite, two-dimensional grid.

To begin with, each cell is either alive or dead.

Once the grid has been set up, three rules are applied to it repeatedly.

1: Any living cell with 2 or 3 adjacent living neighbours stays alive.

2: Any living cell with fewer than 2 or more than 3 adjacent living neighbours dies.

3: Any dead cell with exactly 3 adjacent living neighbours comes to life.

After these three rules have been applied to all cells in the grid, an iteration is complete.

The game will now perform the same operations on this new configuration of cells.
